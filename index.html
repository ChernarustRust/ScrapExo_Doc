Section 1 — Overview

ScrapExo is a tiered exosuit system for Rust. Players activate a powered exoskeleton that replaces their standard armor and provides enhanced combat, mobility, survivability, and drone support. The suit is powered by a battery and fuel system. All features scale across 5 configurable tiers.

Core Impacts:

  • Equips a full multi-layer suit (NVG, diving tank, and other attire) that provides passive protection — all damage is absorbed by the suit's armor bar before your health is touched
  • Provides enhanced movement speed on land, in water, and optionally in flight (Exothrust)
  • Grants automated medical support (auto-syringe, auto-bandage)
  • Provides climate control (heater/AC) to prevent cold/heat damage
  • Includes a fire weapon (longsword variant) and a forcefield item as belt tools
  • Tiers 3-5 support autonomous combat/scouting drones
  • Enhanced HUD shows compass-style directional markers for threats, teammates, bases, charging stations, and waypoints, plus telemetry (ALT, AGL, SPD)
  • Suit state persists across disconnects and is restored on reconnect

What This Replaces

ScrapExo replaces or supersedes the following vanilla Rust mechanics:

  • Armor system — multi-layer suit (NVG, diving tank, and various wear items) stacked in clothing slots, providing passive resistance to damage and environmental hazards
  • Healing items — auto-syringe and auto-bandage trigger automatically
  • Temperature survival — built-in heater and AC manage climate passively
  • Mobility enhancements — speed boost, flight, and swim improvements replace vanilla movement
  • Combat support — autonomous drones replace the need for manual combat tools

ScrapExo functions as a full gameplay system layer rather than a standalone utility plugin, integrating combat, mobility, automation, and UI into a unified experience.

Quick Start

  1. Run /exo1 (or /exo5 for full feature testing)
  2. Wait for the startup sequence (~13 seconds) — movement is reduced to a slow crawl while the suit powers up
  3. Press Ctrl + Space to enable Exothrust flight
  4. Use W / A / S / D to fly, Space to ascend, Ctrl to descend
  5. Press E to toggle altitude hold while flying
  6. Use /exodrone to manage drones (Tier 3+)
  7. Use /exorefill to refill battery and fuel instantly (if permitted)

Section 5 — Core Systems

5.1 Exosuit Activation System

Activation is a two-step confirmation process or instant via activation item:

  • Confirmation: Run /exo# twice — the first press shows the resource cost, the second confirms and activates.
  • Auto-Equip: Suit equips custom armor, saves original wear/belt, and locks slots.
  • Startup: 13.5-second boot sequence with diagnostic messages. Speed boosts are removed during this window — you can still move but only at a slow unassisted pace.
  • Alternative: Place a matching-skin metal.shield in the backpack slot.
  • Deactivation: Run /exo# again for a 10-second shutdown sequence.

5.2 Armor System

The suit is made up of multiple layered wear items (NVG, diving tank, and other attire) all stacked into the clothing slots at once. Together they provide passive protection against damage and environmental hazards. The Armor Bar on your HUD represents the combined health of these layers — when it hits zero, the suit is destroyed. Your personal health is not affected as long as armor remains.

  • All damage is absorbed by the armor bar before touching your health.
  • Hit effects play on every strike. When the armor bar fully depletes, a debris burst effect always plays.
  • Fall damage is reduced by 50% (default).
  • If any bleeding occurs (e.g. during initial suit-up), it is automatically cleared within a fraction of a second.

5.3 Climate Control System

  • Heater: Activates < 15°C, targets 32°C.
  • AC: Activates > 38°C, targets 28°C.
  • Drains: Battery.
  • Cycle: 30-second minimum prevent rapid switching.

5.4 Speed / Movement System

The suit gives you a speed boost that scales up per tier. The game's built-in anti-cheat is suppressed to allow this enhanced movement without triggering false kicks.

5.5 Auto-Medical System

The suit monitors your health and automatically uses medical items from your inventory. This is most useful during the startup phase before the suit is fully powered on.

  • Auto-Syringe: If your health drops below 50, the suit automatically uses a syringe from your inventory. Costs battery.
  • Auto-Bandage: If bleeding is detected, the suit automatically applies a bandage. Costs battery. (Once the suit is fully active, bleeding is suppressed passively and auto-bandage acts as a backup safety net.)
  • Cooldown: 10 seconds between auto-syringe uses, 5 seconds between auto-bandage uses.

5.6 Night Vision System

Tiers 2–5 F to enable night vision goggles when the suit activates. They are removed when the suit shuts down.

5.7 Fire Weapon System

A custom fire longsword is placed in your belt. It shoots heat projectiles that deal fire damage and can damage structures. Each shot consumes fuel.

5.8 Forcefield System

A forcefield item is placed in your belt. When activated, it projects a visible protective sphere around you that absorbs incoming damage. The sphere flashes red whenever it takes a hit. While the forcefield is held in your hand, shooting is disabled. Drains battery for the duration it's active.

Section 7 — Drone System (Detailed)

7.1 Drone Behavior States

At any given time, each drone is in one of the following modes:

  • Hovering: Orbiting around you, waiting for something to happen.
  • Going to base: Heading to your selected base to recharge through the hatch or await orders.
  • Holding at waypoint: Hovering in place above a saved waypoint until recalled.
  • Returning to player: Flying back to you after a recall command.
  • Landing / Ascending: Performing the hatch docking sequence — centering above the door and dropping in, or climbing back out after charging.
  • Charging: Sitting on the DiscoFloor inside your base, refilling its battery.
  • Chasing a target: Actively pursuing and attacking an enemy.
  • Sync lost: Owner has disconnected — drone wanders until the player reconnects.

7.2 Drone Management Panel

Opened with /exodrone. Requires Tier 3+ exosuit. The panel has two columns: your authorized bases on the left and your saved waypoints on the right. During suit shutdown/deactivation, drone commands are locked until shutdown completes.

Drone Selector

A row of buttons appears at the top of the panel showing each active drone (D1, D2, D3...) plus an ALL button. Click a D# to target that specific drone for your next action. Click ALL to apply actions to every drone at once. Selecting a drone that is unavailable or destroyed shows a warning and keeps the current selection unchanged. A chat confirmation ([DRONE] Selected: D1) is sent each time you make a selection so you can verify what the system registered. Action buttons (Deploy, Recall, Recall All) have a short cooldown to prevent accidental double-firing from rapid clicks.

Actions

  • Deploy to Target: Sends the selected drone(s) to whichever base or waypoint is highlighted yellow. Drones actively charging on their pad are protected and cannot be redirected — drones still in transit can be overridden immediately.
  • Recall: Brings a specific selected drone (D1, D2...) back to you. Requires a D# to be selected — not available when ALL is active.
  • Recall All: Brings every drone back to orbit around you and clears all base/waypoint assignments.
  • Storage: Opens the first available drone's cargo inventory.
  • Spawn: Spawns a new drone. Without scrapexo.bypass.drone, a drone item is consumed from your inventory. With the bypass permission, drones are free to spawn. Shows a warning if no item is available or permission is missing. Alternatively, dropping a drone item from your inventory while the suit is active will also spawn it directly. If the chassis has a depleted battery, you must be standing within 3m of a powered charging station — the drone will spawn directly onto the pad and begin charging at the normal rate, activating automatically once full. Safety lock: spawn from panel and drop-to-activate are both blocked while suit shutdown/deactivation is active.
  • Despawn: Recalls all active drones. Each drone flies back to your position, lands nearby, then returns its chassis to your inventory. Players with scrapexo.bypass.drone have their drones simply deactivate on landing — no item is returned since none was spent.

Waypoints in the Panel

Waypoints saved with /wpmark appear in the right column of the drone panel. Selecting one and clicking Deploy to Target sends the selected drone(s) there. Drones circle above the waypoint until recalled — they do not automatically return on their own unless battery depletes.

7.3 Charging Behavior (Hatch Docking)

  1. When a drone's battery is low (<30), it diverts to a charging station if one is in range.
  2. It navigates to the floor-frame hatch above the charging area, aligns itself over the opening, and opens the hatch.
  3. The drone descends through the hatch into the base interior, moves to an available DiscoFloor charging pad, positions itself correctly, and begins charging.
  4. After reaching full battery, it ascends back through the hatch and returns to the player.

If multiple drones return at the same time, they queue their entry — each drone waits until the hatch and charging area are clear before proceeding to avoid collisions.

Low-battery drones cancel current travel tasks (base/waypoint/chase) and divert to charging. If no charging station is available, the drone returns to the operator for retrieval.

If a drone reaches 0 battery before retrieval, it crashes in-world (no instant inventory teleport). The suit console reports the grid and relative direction from the operator at signal loss.

Lost-drone tracking above the armor bar is intentionally disabled; operators rely on the console warning/grid callout for recovery.

Battery state is remembered. When a drone is retrieved its remaining battery level is stored on the chassis item. A drone returned with a depleted battery can only be reactivated by standing within 3m of a powered charging station and using Spawn or dropping the item — the chassis will be placed directly on the pad and charge at the normal rate before automatically taking off.

Important: DiscoFloor charging pads require an active power source to function. Charging drones and the exosuit both draw from connected batteries — make sure your base power supply is sufficient to support both.

Section 8 — Resource Management

8.1 Battery System

  • Drains during use + Climate Control + Auto-med.
  • Forcefield adds per-second drain.
  • 0 Battery: Speed boost removed — player moves slowly as if wearing heavy unpowered armor. Flight shuts down.
  • Refill: DiscoFloor, dropping small battery items, /exorefill.

8.2 Fuel System

Fuel is used for: Exothrust (flight) and Fire Weapon (longsword attacks)

  • Drains during Exothrust flight.
  • Fire weapon shots multiply fuel consumption.
  • 0 Fuel: Automatic flight shutdown.
  • Refill: Drop lowgradefuel, /exorefill.

8.3 Charging Station (DiscoFloor)

Standing on a DiscoFloor recharges your suit battery. Higher tiers charge faster. You need to be standing directly on the floor to get the charge — hovering just above it won't count.

Deployment: Players can deploy their own charging stations by placing a Disco Rug (Skin 10006). Upon placement, the rug is automatically converted into a functional DiscoFloor entity. These can be distributed via Kits or other item spawning plugins.

Section 9 — UI / HUD

9.1 Status Bars

Armor, Battery, and Fuel bars on left. Auto-shift upward to avoid overlapping vanilla status effects.

9.2 Enhanced HUD (Tiers 3–5)

Compass updated every 1s. Markers for Drones (Yellow), Charging Stations (Cyan), Threats (Red), Teammates (Green), Base, and Waypoints.

9.3 Telemetry Panel

  • [ALT]: Altitude (MSL).
  • [AGL]: Height via raycast (Color-coded: Red < 10ft, Yellow < 30ft).
  • [SPD]: Speed (Color-coded: Cyan > 3m/s, Orange > 10m/s).

Section 13 — Technical Notes

Key Classes

  • ExoData: Main per-player state container.
  • ExoDrone: MonoBehaviour (16 Hz update) for drone AI.
  • ExothrustComponent: Physics handler for flight.
  • MovableBaseMountable: Custom mountable to prevent dismounting.

Performance

  • Content-hash caching for all UI redraws.
  • Raycasts limited to 1 per second for Telemetry.
  • Facepunch.Pool used for all entity lists.

Section 14 — Configuration Notes

Quick overview: Most gameplay behavior is configurable from the plugin config without code edits. You can tune balance, UI behavior, automation, and progression for your server style.

  • Tier balancing: armor values, battery/fuel capacities, drain rates, speed multipliers, flight/swim boosts, and feature unlocks per tier.
  • Drone systems: max drones per tier, chase/hover ranges, charging behavior, battery thresholds, and turret enable/scaling.
  • Combat & survivability: forcefield settings, fire weapon behavior, healing automation, damage effects, and fall-damage handling.
  • HUD/UI: radar, console, and bar behavior/positions; visibility rules; update cadence and status presentation.
  • Resource economy: item costs, refill behavior, charging rates, and conversion/consumption pacing.
  • Admin controls: permissions, bypass rules, diagnostics/debug toggles, and command access boundaries.

Skin Ownership (UseSkinOwnership)

Controls how custom skins are applied to suit items (armor pieces, belt tools, etc.):

  • true (recommended): Skins are only applied if the player owns them. Non-owned skins fall back to default appearance.
  • false: Forces skins regardless of ownership. May not work with all DLC/paid skins and is not recommended.
